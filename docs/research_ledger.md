| Forced-flow announcement→effective continuation (S&P 500 additions) | **Rejected — leg closed** | Preregistered, return-blind test run **once** on the frozen 75-batch corpus (manifest sha256 `152622d8…`); 61 executable batches (14 recent **spinoff** additions non-executable — no pre-effective trading). Gross announcement→effective residual **+126 bps** (median +70, borderline: CI [−25,+308]) — index effect exists but modest/noisy. **Net after 15 bps/side hedged costs: +59 bps mean, median +1 bps, CI [−96,+240] includes 0** (P>0=0.76) — not significant; **fails doubled-cost** (net −7 bps) and bootstrap lower bound <0. Placebo (same names, random windows) is **+30 bps**, so part of the gross is survivorship/momentum, not the announcement. **REJECT, close the leg, no rescue filters.** Reject-only at n=61 anyway (promotion needs ≥100). Consistent with the disappearing-index-effect literature (NBER w30748). `scripts/forced_flow_continuation_test.py`, `data/forced_flow_continuation_test.json`, trial `forced_flow_continuation_test_v1`. |
| Crypto OFI flow-absorption (5-min, aggTrades) | **Rejected — no flow edge** | 89 days BTC+ETH real signed order flow (free Binance aggTrades). **OFI → next-5/15/30-min return correlation ≈ 0** (0.002/0.007/0.008); elastic-net **zeroes the OFI coefficient**; predicted edges <3 bps vs a 32 bps (2× round-trip) cost gate → **0 tradeable executions**; **always-on gross day-Sharpe NEGATIVE** (−1.5 to −1.8) — no gross signal even before costs. | Crypto TSMOM (BTC/ETH perps, daily trend) | **Shortlisted candidate — first to clear the full backtest battery** | Real Binance data 2019→2026. **Net Sharpe 1.07** after taker fees + funding (~3.2–3.8 bps/day, modeled) + slippage; **positive in all 4 regimes** (2019-21 1.70, 2022 0.43, 2023-24 0.93, 2025-26 0.75); survives **doubled-cost 1.00** and **delayed-entry 1.01**; bootstrap Sharpe LB 0.31. **Caveats (loud):** replication of a documented effect, NOT novel alpha; the >1 is partly 2019-21 bull-carried (equal-weight-regime avg **0.95**, 2022 only 0.43); wide CI [0.31, 1.88]; it is a **daily TREND OVERLAY, not the intraday day-trader**; still needs **Deflated Sharpe + a real forward paper record + per-year/asset leave-one-out** before promotion; **paper-only, no capital**. `scripts/crypto_tsmom_backtest.py`, trial `crypto_tsmom_btc_eth_v1`. |
| Diversified time-series (trend) momentum on ETF proxies | Diagnostic: modest positive, below standalone gate | Survivorship-immune 13-ETF cross-asset basket (equity/rates/credit/commodity/RE), monthly rebalance, vol-targeted, frozen. **Net Sharpe 0.49–0.66** (combined 0.66 at 2 bps, **0.63 at 5 bps — cost-robust**), max DD −9%, **corr to SPY 0.04–0.26 (diversifying)**, 2012→2026. Does **not** clear the >1 standalone Sharpe gate — a faithful replication of TSMOM's post-2011 decay, not a discovery. **Robustness stress-test (`tsmom_etf_stress_v1`, not new variants) PASSES:** survives delayed entry (0.59 @6d), stable across all three sub-periods (0.87/0.45/0.69), positive 11/14 years, **not 2022-concentrated** (ex-2022 Sharpe 0.65), PSR(true Sharpe>0)=0.99 far above the deflated null benchmark (E[max\|null]=0.10 for 8 configs). But PSR(true Sharpe>0.5)=0.70 and it is <1 gate. **Conclusion: the one signal in this repo that survives honest scrutiny — a real, cost/delay/regime-robust MODEST diversifying overlay (low SPY corr 0.04–0.26), not standalone alpha.** `scripts/tsmom_etf_diag.py`, `scripts/tsmom_etf_stress.py`, trials `tsmom_etf_diag_v1`/`tsmom_etf_stress_v1`. |
| SEC Atlas signed reaction-drift (taxonomy-sign form) | Discovery null; auto-tags directionally unreliable | Frozen return-blind structural-sign map (`atlas-structural-sign-v1`) tested on the 490 signed, current-linked (survivorship-biased) priced discovery events. **No family shows significant tradeable post-open drift** (all 5-day bootstrap CIs cross zero). Worse, the *immediate* signed gap is **wrong-signed** for the largest "positive" auto-tag families: `guidance_raised` −65 bps, `major_customer_win` −54 bps, `activist_13d` −84 bps (market moved opposite the structural prior). Only negative-news families where "bad news→down" is hard to mis-tag carry the correct sign (secondary_offering, restatement, cyber, going_concern), and even they have no significant drift. **Confirms the directive's crux** ("predicts something moves, not which way") and shows **human directional annotation is a hard prerequisite** — signed tests on auto-tags are invalid. `scripts/atlas_signed_drift_diag.py`, `data/atlas_signed_drift_diag.json`, trial `atlas_signed_drift_diag_v1`. Not a kill: proper annotation of the correctly-signed negative-news families may still be worth one test. |
